Childhood

I grew up without a stable home because my parents were always chasing business opportunities. I’ve been through so much; we moved from Cotabato to Zamboanga, Cagayan, and Davao, before getting stuck in Lanao del Sur. Even though we had acquaintances there, we stayed for six years long enough to get caught in the middle of the war and then the quarantine. It took a serious toll on my mental health. I can honestly say my childhood wasn't happy, and I never really learned how to socialize with people.

Teenage Life

My teenage years followed the same pattern. we moved again, which is in Lanao Del Norte, I spent most of my time indoors, and online gaming and chatting became my primary source of joy. Because my parents were so strict, my life revolved entirely around school and home. I chose to be obedient just to avoid their anger, which left me feeling like I didn't have much of a life outside of the house.

Adulthood

Now that I’m an adult, I finally feel a sense of freedom. I’m finally experiencing the things I’ve always wanted to do. I’ve made friends, gone through heartbreaks, tried drinkingI’ve just been exploring new things. I’m really proud of how far I’ve come and the person I’m becoming today.
